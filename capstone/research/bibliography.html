Capstone Project: Annotated Bibliography

American Psychological Association: Artificial intelligence and adolescent well-being

This article talks about both the positives and negatives of AI usage on adolescent minds. It delves into recommendations for how young children should use AI as well as the possible concerns it could bring. The article also discusses the impact that having a relationship with article intelligence can have on a developing cognition, as well as what educators, policymakers, and tech developers should do to prevent some of the negative side effects. This article will be essential to my project because I will be talking about a dystopian future where AI usage could lead to many negative side effects.

Time Magazine: ChatGPT May Be Eroding Critical Thinking Skills, According to a New MIT Study

This article discusses a MIT study that asked participants to write SAT essays using either ChatGPT, Google, and nothing at all. The article concluded that the ChatGPT users' brains had the lowest brain engagement and consistently underperformed. By the end of the study, the ChatGPT users got lazy and often just copy-and-pasted answers from AI. I think this article will probably be the most helpful to me in terms of informational research.

Pew Research Center: Views of AI's impact on society and human abilities

This report describes their polling results on American sentiment about AI. Most Americans remain more concerned about AI than excited, and the majority do not want AI involved in their personal matters. However, many want AI to play a small role in society. I think this report will be good to add to the story of my project because it talks about real reactions from random people about AI, not just professors or tech developers.
